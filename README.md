# Austo Motor Company Customer Analysis

## Project Overview
This project looks at Austo Motor Company’s customer data to find purchasing patterns, financial behaviour, demographic trends, and vehicle preferences. The goal is to support marketing strategies based on data, improve customer experience, and increase campaign effectiveness.

## Business Problem
Austo Motor Company noticed declining returns from its marketing campaigns. The analysis focuses on:

- Identifying high-value customer segments
- Understanding price sensitivity among customer groups
- Analysing customer preference for SUVs, Sedans, and Hatchbacks
- Recommending targeted marketing and product strategies

## Dataset
The dataset contains customer-level information such as:

- Age
- Gender
- Profession
- Marital status
- Education
- Number of dependents
- Personal loan and house loan status
- Partner working status
- Salary, partner salary, and total salary
- Car price
- Car type or make

The dataset includes 1,581 customer records and 14 attributes.

## Key Insights
- High-income female customers showed a stronger preference for SUVs.
- Salaried professionals preferred SUVs more than business professionals.
- Budget-conscious families and customers with more dependents favoured Hatchbacks.
- Customers with personal loans typically spent less on cars.
- Older customers, especially those aged 35 and older, showed higher purchasing power and a preference for premium models.
- Dual-income households purchased more frequently, but not necessarily more expensive cars.

## Business Recommendations
- Promote SUVs to high-income female customers by highlighting luxury, safety, and status.
- Market Hatchbacks to budget-conscious families by focusing on affordability and fuel efficiency.
- Target salaried male customers with Sedan campaigns that emphasize reliability and family use.
- Offer flexible EMI or low-interest financing options for customers sensitive to loans.
- Stock more SUVs for high-income customer segments.
- Launch loyalty programs and discount packages aimed at families.
